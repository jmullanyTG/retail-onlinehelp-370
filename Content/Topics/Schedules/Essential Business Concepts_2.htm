Essential Business Concepts

How shipping and receiving schedules are applied to both stores and DCs will affect ordering behavior in different ways. Not all locations will require specific schedule sets, but they may be used to restrict how order dates are produced by the JustEnough Replenishment Engine when the desired behavior is to align ordering with logistical constraints. JustEnough is, at its core, an inventory optimization tool; application and integration related any real-world logistical constraints may hinder sales, stocking levels, or general availability.

  • Receiving Schedule – The receiving schedule determines the dates on which a store or DC may receive stock. This may be set to ensure busy stores receive stock shipments during times with less foot traffic, or when staff is available for processing.
  • Shipping Schedule – Shipping schedules will determine the dates on which an order may be placed against a store or DC. It is assumed that any location is able to ship on the same day as an order may be placed. In cases where there is a lag in time between order placement and actual shipping of goods, an additional day should be added to the lead time to represent this lag.
